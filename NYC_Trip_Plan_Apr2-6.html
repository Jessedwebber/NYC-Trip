Day 2 — History & Downtown

Friday, April 3
8:30 AM

Breakfast: Tompkins Square Bagels $8–12

165 Avenue A, East Village. Hand-rolled, kettle-boiled. Everything bagel with scallion cream cheese. One of NYC's best.

Subway 6 from 51st St → Astor Pl · walk 10 min east · ~20 min total
10:00 AM

Tenement Museum $30

103 Orchard St, Lower East Side. Guided walking tour through preserved 19th-century tenement apartments where immigrant families lived. Hear the real stories of Irish, German, Italian, and Jewish families who built New York. Tours 60–90 min, deeply immersive. Book timed tour at tenement.org — they sell out.

Walk 15 min south from East Village to LES
12:00 PM

Lunch: Katz's Delicatessen $25–30

205 E Houston St, LES. Open since 1888. The most famous deli in America — hand-carved pastrami on rye (featured in When Harry Met Sally). Sandwiches ~$28 and enormous. Take a ticket at the door, don't lose it!

Walk 5 min from Tenement Museum
1:15 PM

Subway to Lower Manhattan $3

Take the F from Delancey St → Bowling Green. ~15 min.

1:30 PM

National Museum of the American Indian Free

1 Bowling Green, Lower Manhattan. Smithsonian branch in the stunning 1907 Alexander Hamilton Custom House. Always free. Exhibits on Indigenous cultures across the Americas. Usually uncrowded — a hidden gem most tourists walk past.

3:00 PM

Brooklyn Bridge Walk Free

Walk from City Hall across the Brooklyn Bridge to DUMBO. ~30 min walk with spectacular skyline views. Grab the famous Washington Street photo framing the Manhattan Bridge in DUMBO.

Walk 10 min north from Bowling Green to bridge entrance
4:30 PM

Subway to West Village $3

From DUMBO: walk to York St station (F train) → W 4th St–Washington Sq. ~15 min.

5:00 PM

Walk the High Line Free

Enter at Gansevoort St, walk north. 1.45-mile elevated park on a disused 1930s freight rail line. Public art, native plantings, architecture views. Free.

6:30 PM

Dinner: Carbone $60–80

181 Thompson St, Greenwich Village. Italian-American fine dining. Famous spicy vodka rigatoni and veal parmesan. Try for a walk-in at the bar. If unavailable, Don Angie ($45–65) or Via Carota ($40–55) are nearby alternatives.

Walk 10 min from High Line south entrance
9:00 PM

Drinks: PDT (Please Don't Tell) $50–65

113 St Marks Pl, East Village. Enter through a vintage phone booth inside Crif Dogs hot dog shop. Pick up the phone — if there's space, the back wall opens. Cocktails $19–24. ~20 seats. Reservations on Resy at 3 PM daily. Grab a Crif Dog ($5) while you wait.

Walk 15 min east from W 4th to St Marks Pl · or subway 6 from Bleecker St → Astor Pl

The Metropolitan Museum of Art $30

1000 5th Ave at 82nd St. Don't-miss highlights: the Egyptian Temple of Dendur, American Wing, Arms & Armor, and European paintings (Vermeer, Rembrandt). Even 2.5 hours here is transformative. $30 adults. Book at metmuseum.org.
